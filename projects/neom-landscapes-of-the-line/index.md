# NEOM: Landscapes of The Line
**Year:** 2022–2025
**Type:** Territorial Design Consultation
**Location:** NEOM, Saudi Arabia
**Collaborators:** Adam Mekies, Sherwood Design Engineers
**Phase:** Phase IV — Cultivated Wildness (2017–2025)

## Description

170-kilometer linear urban development traversing desert terrain from mountains to coast. The manifold concept: The Line as infrastructure concentrating and redistributing water from north to south, channeling water to catalyze habitats and carve geologies. The Wadis: traditional ephemeral desert watercourses reconceived as holding areas. Internet of ecologies: interconnected network of biodiversity hotspots, ecological corridors, and migration routes relaying real-time data through local sensors and remote monitoring. GeoHECRAS hydrological modeling revealed that conventional channelization would require widths exceeding 200 meters, driving the development of hybrid approaches managing wild waters instead.

## Contribution

From form to process at territorial scale: developing strategies for initiating and guiding processes including erosion, deposition, vegetation establishment, and ecological succession that would unfold over generations. Managed complexity: extends geomorphology table research to territorial application. The Line as experiment: the northern landscapes as an experimental laboratory where the GeoHECRAS finding became the technical moment that made adaptive alternatives necessary.
