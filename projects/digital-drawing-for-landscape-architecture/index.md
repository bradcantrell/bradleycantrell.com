# Digital Drawing for Landscape Architecture
**Year:** 2010
**Type:** Publication
**Collaborators:** Wes Michaels
**Publisher:** Wiley
**Recognition:** 2012 ASLA Award of Excellence in Communication
**Phase:** Phase I — The Digital Threshold (2005–2012)

## Description

A methodology for digital representation calibrated to landscape's specific disciplinary concerns. Core contribution: the digital composite, layering heterogeneous information including texture, shadow, vegetation, atmospheric effect, and temporal change that no single software engine can generate. The hybrid workflow moved from hand-drawn linework through image-editing software to layered photographic textures, rendered 3D passes, and digital painting. Effectively standardized a new visual language for the profession.

## Contribution

Representation is never neutral: the way a landscape architect draws a site determines how they understand it. Vector lines in CAD imply hard edges while landscape materials are defined by indeterminacy. Introducing techniques that hold ambiguity and atmospheric depth equipped the discipline to represent and therefore think about higher levels of complexity.
