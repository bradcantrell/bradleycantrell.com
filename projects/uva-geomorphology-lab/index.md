# UVA Geomorphology Lab and Adaptive Design Protocols
**Year:** 2017–present
**Type:** Research Infrastructure / Pedagogical Platform
**Location:** UVA School of Architecture
**Collaborators:** Brian Davis, Xun Liu
**Phase:** Phase III — Codification (2016–2020)

## Description

REAL methods codified into teachable pedagogy at UVA. Larger EmRiver table with the same sensing infrastructure including Kinect 2, ultrasonic banner sensors on stepper-motor-driven Arduino-controlled rails, image analysis, and dye visualization. Machine learning integration explored how sediment gates might develop response patterns through learned optimization rather than pre-programmed rules, drawing the analogy between an early thermostat and a Nest that learns from context.

## Contribution

Composite modeling as teachable methodology: experimental practice transmitted across generations of students and researchers. From responsivity to autonomy via machine learning: infrastructure that continually learns from itself by processing the results of its actions before choosing the next intervention. Collapsing design phases: representation, analysis, documentation, and construction into continuous feedback.
