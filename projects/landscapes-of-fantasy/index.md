# Landscapes of Fantasy
**Year:** 2008
**Type:** Pedagogical Research / Curriculum
**Location:** LSU Robert Reich School of Landscape Architecture
**Phase:** Phase I — The Digital Threshold (2005–2012)

## Description

Kim Stanley Robinson's Red Mars and other science fiction novels provided descriptive passages that students translated into visual media through five exercise typologies: Diorama/Scene, Object/Device, Environment/Context/Surface, Dynamics/Interaction, and speculative terrain. No photographic reference was available because the landscapes did not exist. Students learned to construct environmental imagination from language alone.

## Contribution

Imagination precedes representation. Displacing students from known landscapes broke habitual reliance on photographic reference and visual tropes. Temporal coherence became a representational obligation, and the shift from object to field, from idealization to expression of experience, became teachable method. Extended Thresholds from installation to pedagogy.
