# Responsive Landscapes
**Year:** 2016
**Type:** Publication
**Collaborators:** Justine Holzman
**Publisher:** Routledge
**Phase:** Phase II — The Operative Landscape (2012–2016)

## Description

Six-mode taxonomy of how technology might mediate between environmental process and human experience: Elucidate (revealing invisible processes), Compress (condensing vast spatial/temporal scales), Displace (telepresence), Connect (networked systems linking disparate agents), Ambient (ubiquitous background monitoring), Modify (active systems physically altering the environment in real time). Drew on REAL and Sedimachine research.

## Contribution

Technology in landscape is not merely about efficiency but about phenomenology, changing how humans perceive and interact with environmental systems. Responsiveness is not a single condition but a spectrum of engagements. Landscape reconceived not as passive recipient of design intervention but as active participant in ongoing cycles of perception and response.
