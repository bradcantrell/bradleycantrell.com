# Algorithmic Cultivation
**Year:** 2019
**Type:** Installation / Manifest
**Location:** UVA School of Architecture
**Collaborators:** Lucia Phinney, Robin Dripps, Emma Mendel
**Phase:** Phase III — Codification (2016–2020)

## Description

A gantry robot moved over plants in custom acrylic planters, executing shearing operations derived from remote datasets including environmental data and species migration patterns having no direct relationship to the growing environment. Unlike conventional hedge maintenance, the robot could execute frequent cuts in varying planes, producing growth patterns impossible through manual cultivation. Conceived to run for a year, technical difficulties limited operation to weeks. Situated in three converging crises: pandemic emergence at urban-wildlife interfaces, species migration under climate change, and the transformation of agriculture through automation.

## Contribution

Data as medium, not optimization: the data operated as an unfiltered random catalyst for provoking the feedback loop of stimulus and response, severing the instrumental relationship between data and outcome. Interspecies communication as design proposition: pruning and growth as proto-linguistic exchange. The Third Intelligence prototype: staged encounter between human intention, machine cognition, and biological agency.
