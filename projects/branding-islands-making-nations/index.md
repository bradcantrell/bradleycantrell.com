# Branding Islands Making Nations
**Year:** 2016
**Type:** Speculative Exhibition / Film Installation
**Location:** 15th Venice Architecture Biennale
**Collaborators:** Vertical Geopolitics Lab
**Phase:** Phase II — The Operative Landscape (2012–2016)

## Description

Exhibition and public program examining the relationship between physical land creation and the representational language deployed to legitimize it. A film on how production of new land intersects with production of meaning. Large-format prints documenting how branding and marketing operate as instruments of geopolitical power. An invited competition asked marketing experts and communication designers to develop branding packages for artificial landmasses in the South China Sea. The competition deliberately suspended moral evaluation. The goal was exposure, not endorsement.

## Contribution

Representation as territory: constructed land acquires political existence through representational practices as much as through material deposition. The designer's complicity: the capacity to choreograph land-making processes is also the capacity to enable territorial expansion, displacement, and conflict. Satire as method: treating geopolitical branding as a legitimate design problem defamiliarized practices that typically operate invisibly.
