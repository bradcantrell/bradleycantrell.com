# Designing Autonomy
**Year:** 2017
**Type:** Publication / Theoretical Framework
**Collaborators:** Laura J. Martin, Erle C. Ellis
**Publisher:** Trends in Ecology & Evolution 32(3): 156–166
**Phase:** Phase IV — Cultivated Wildness (2017–2025)

## Description

Published in a leading ecology journal, bringing landscape architecture's responsive technologies into dialogue with conservation biology, rewilding discourse, and AI research. A two-axis diagram mapped wildness against human influence, revealing these need not be inversely related. Four automation levels proposed increasing toward deep reinforcement learning for all levels of decision and action. The wildness creator: autonomous landscape infrastructure whose operations would become unrecognizable and incomprehensible to human beings, producing ecological patterns divergent from anything humans created.

## Contribution

Redefining wildness: wild reconceived as relative freedom from human interventions, a condition that could paradoxically be produced and maintained through technological mediation. The machine as gardener: inverts Leo Marx's machine in the garden. Distanced authorship: design that favors process, curation, and choreography over direct formal specification. Deep reinforcement learning as conservation method.
