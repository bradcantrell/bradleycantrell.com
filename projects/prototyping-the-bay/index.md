# Prototyping the Bay
**Year:** 2021–2025
**Type:** Pedagogical Research / Design Studio
**Location:** UVA, LAR 7020 Foundation Studio IV
**Collaborators:** Leena Cho (co-instructor)
**Recognition:** Tulane Climate Curriculum Prize, 2025
**Phase:** Phase IV — Cultivated Wildness (2017–2025)

## Description

Fourth-semester foundation studio. The Chesapeake Bay, contaminated, slowly submerging, ecologically disrupted, as both subject and instrument. Focus: Pocomoke Sound's coastal landscapes and islands, where sea-level rise, land subsidence, and shifting ecological communities create radical uncertainty. Three modules moved from research and mapping through systems diagrams and site prototypes to comprehensive design strategies treating the Bay as dynamic landscape shaped by human, non-human, geologic, historic, biologic, political, and hydrological agents.

## Contribution

From prediction to adaptation: the studio explicitly rejects restoring or saving the Chesapeake, asking instead how designers engage rapidly changing environments without assuming predictive capacity. Landscape as knowledge production: sites as testing grounds that produce experiments identifying thresholds that drive ecological responses. Values-based design: the first course objective insists that engagement with dynamic systems requires explicit ethical positioning.
