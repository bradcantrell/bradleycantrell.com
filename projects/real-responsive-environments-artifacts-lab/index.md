# REAL — Responsive Environments and Artifacts Lab
**Year:** 2014–2017
**Type:** Research Infrastructure / Manifest
**Location:** Harvard GSD
**Collaborators:** Allen Sayegh (co-director)
**Phase:** Phase II — The Operative Landscape (2012–2016)

## Description

EmRiver geomorphology table augmented with sensing systems. Synthetic sediment producing stratigraphic patterns. Programmable hydrographs, adjustable bed slope, groundwater pump. Kinect overhead for continuous point clouds, ultrasonic range finders on motorized rails, image analysis, dye visualization. Responsive prototypes: robotic sediment gates, sieves, flow disruptors. Three engagement modes spanning direct interaction through responsivity to autonomy. The table was conceived as its own environment, not a scaled replica of any particular landscape.

## Contribution

Composite modeling as design methodology: coupling physical and numerical models in feedback. Model-as-site vs. model-as-scaled-replica: the table enabled discovery of principles of interaction rather than validation against a prototype. From responsivity to autonomy: a gradient that maps increasing machine agency. Collapsing design phases: representation, analysis, documentation, and construction into continuous feedback.
