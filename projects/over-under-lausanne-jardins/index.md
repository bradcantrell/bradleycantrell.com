# Over/Under: Lausanne Jardins
**Year:** 2009
**Type:** Speculative Design / Competition
**Location:** Lausanne, Switzerland
**Collaborators:** Allen Sayegh (INVIVIA), Edith K. Ackermann, Marcella Del Signore
**Phase:** Phase I — The Digital Threshold (2005–2012)

## Description

A single specimen citrus tree occupied a plaza above a subway station, sustained by visible hydroponic infrastructure, an explicit homage to the 17th-century orangery tradition where exotic species were kept alive through artificial life-support. Below in the subway station, the specimen was digitally replicated and transformed into a shifting forest of projections across station walls, responding to the living plant's seasonal changes. Commuters moving underground encountered a rich botanical environment derived from the living plant above.

## Contribution

Environmental displacement as design strategy: experience of a landscape condition can be detached from physical presence in that landscape and reconstructed elsewhere through technological mediation, not as simulation but as transformation. The digital forest explicitly exceeded its source. Made visible what urban greenery typically conceals, which is that plants in cities are artifacts of intensive technological mediation.
